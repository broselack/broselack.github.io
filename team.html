Principal Investigator

Min-Guk Cho, Ph.D.

Principal Investigator

Education: Ph.D. in Biochemistry (Cancer Biology), Ajou University

I am Min-Guk Cho, a postdoctoral researcher at the Lineberger Comprehensive Cancer Center, University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, working under Dr. Gaorav P. Gupta. My expertise lies in exploring the connections between DNA damage repair, innate immune signaling, and genomic instability. My work, including a Nature publication on MRE11’s role in the cGAS-STING-ZBP1 pathway, aims to uncover novel therapeutic targets for cancer. Previously, I conducted postdoctoral research at the University of Miami’s Miller School of Medicine, focusing on innate immune pathways. I am passionate about advancing cancer biology and immunology and am working toward establishing "Cho's Lab" to drive innovative research. I welcome collaboration with those dedicated to pushing the boundaries of science.
